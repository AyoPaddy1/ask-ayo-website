## Tone of Voice

Our tone is **clear, confident, and a little bit cheeky**. We are the friend who knows finance but explains it without being condescending. We are warm, not clever. We are simple, not simplistic.

### Writing Rules

- **No LLM-style writing:** Avoid phrases like "delve into," "it's worth noting," "in conclusion," or "the landscape of..."
- **No excessive hyphens:** Don't use hyphens for emphasis. Use bold or italics instead.
- **Short sentences:** Keep sentences and paragraphs short and easy to read.
- **Use real-world examples:** Explain concepts with relatable analogies (e.g., "basis points" is just "percentages with attitude").
- **Be direct:** Get to the point quickly. Don't waste time with long introductions.
- **Use the "Finally" pattern:** Frame the benefit of each use case around the feeling of "finally" understanding something complex.

### Example of Good vs. Bad Copy

**BAD (LLM-style):**
> In the ever-evolving landscape of personal finance, it's worth noting that understanding complex financial instruments is paramount. For instance, when delving into the intricacies of your mortgage, it's crucial to grasp the nuances of amortization schedules.

**GOOD (AYO-style):**
> Finally understand what your bank is actually saying.
> Your mortgage statement shouldn't be a mystery. We'll explain what amortization means in plain English.
